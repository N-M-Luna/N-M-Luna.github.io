About me.

Born in Puerto Rico, I have been bilingual since a young age. Even as a master's student of English with a concentration in Linguistics, I have been maintaining a complementary independent study of Spanish.

My interest in comparative linguistics and translation started from my bilingual upbringing. In college, I took two semesters of Japanese just see such a contrasting language. Little did I know that years later, a Japan Society for the Promotion for Science Fellowship would be sending me to Yamaguchi, Japan for a summer of research.

I love to write in both Spanish and English. I have co-authored two academic papers while pursuing my master's in mathematics. Out of my experience in the mathematics community, came two published essays.

Since I graduated in 2018, I have been lecturing online from Sacramento, CA at the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley. I'm interested in making a career change into the publishing industry.

My hobbies include calligraphy, crochet, and reading.
